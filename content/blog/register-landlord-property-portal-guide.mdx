**TL;DR:** The Renters' Rights Act 2025 requires all private landlords in England to register on the Property Portal by 1 May 2026. You will need your personal details, property information, compliance certificates (gas safety, EICR, EPC), and a PRS Ombudsman membership number. According to the [Property Portal's official user guidance](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/property-portal-landlord-registration-guidance), the process takes 20–30 minutes if documents are ready. Missing the deadline carries [civil penalties of up to £7,000 for a first offence under Section 21 of the Renters' Rights Act 2025](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/section/21) and [can prevent you from serving valid possession notices under Section 8 proceedings](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/section/8).

## The Property Portal Is Here — Time to Register

The Property Portal is one of the most significant practical changes introduced by the [Renters' Rights Act 2025](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents). It is a centralised digital database where every private landlord in England must register themselves and their rental properties — and the registration window is now open.

All information in this guide is sourced from the [official Property Portal user guidance published by MHCLG](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/property-portal-landlord-registration-guidance), the [Renters' Rights Act 2025 on legislation.gov.uk](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents), the [PRS Ombudsman membership requirements](https://www.theprsombudsman.org.uk/landlord-membership), and LandlordReady's own testing of the portal registration process during the beta phase.

If you have been following the build-up, you already know the portal is coming. This guide is about the practical mechanics: what you need to have ready, how the registration process works step by step, and what your ongoing obligations look like once you are registered.

<KeyDate date="1 May 2026" />

The compliance deadline is approaching. If you let residential property in England under an assured tenancy, you must be registered on the Property Portal by this date. There is no grace period, and the consequences of missing it are real.

## What You Will Need Before You Start

The single best thing you can do is gather everything before you sit down to register. Trying to locate certificate numbers and membership details mid-way through the process is a reliable way to turn a 20-minute task into a frustrating afternoon. Here is what you will need.

### Personal and Contact Details

- Your full legal name (or company name, if you let through a limited company)
- Your correspondence address
- A valid email address — this becomes your primary contact for portal notifications
- A telephone number

### Property Information

For each property you let, you will need:

- The full address, including postcode
- The property type (house, flat, HMO, etc.)
- The number of bedrooms
- Current tenancy status and number of occupants

### Compliance Certificates

This is where most landlords need to spend time preparing. For each property, have digital copies and reference numbers ready for:

- **[Gas safety certificate](/blog/gas-safety-certificate-landlord-guide-2026)** — the certificate number, date of issue, and expiry date
- **[Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)](/blog/electrical-safety-certificate-landlord-requirements)** — the report reference number and outcome (satisfactory or unsatisfactory)
- **[Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)](/blog/epc-rating-requirements-rental-property-2026)** — the RRN (Report Reference Number) from the EPC register, along with the current rating

### PRS Ombudsman Membership

You must be a member of the [Private Rented Sector Ombudsman](/blog/prs-ombudsman-landlord-membership-requirements) before you register on the portal. [The Property Portal requires a valid PRS Ombudsman membership number as a mandatory field](https://www.theprsombudsman.org.uk/landlord-membership) — the portal will not let you complete registration without it.

<Callout variant="warning">If you have not yet joined the PRS Ombudsman, do this first. You cannot complete your Property Portal registration without a valid membership number. Our guide to [PRS Ombudsman membership requirements](/blog/prs-ombudsman-landlord-membership-requirements) explains how to join and what it costs.</Callout>

## How Do I Create My Landlord Account?

The portal has been designed to be straightforward, and for a landlord with one or two properties, the process should take between 20 and 30 minutes — assuming you have your documents ready.

Visit the [Property Portal website](https://www.gov.uk/property-portal) and select **Register as a landlord**. You will be asked to verify your identity through [GOV.UK One Login](https://www.gov.uk/using-your-gov-uk-one-login), the government's single sign-on service. If you have used GOV.UK One Login before — for example, to file a Self Assessment tax return — you can sign in with the same credentials. If not, you will create an account using your email address and a form of photo ID.

Once verified, enter your personal details: your full legal name (or company name if you let through a limited company), your correspondence address, a contact email, and a telephone number. You will also enter your **PRS Ombudsman membership number** at this stage — the portal validates it against the [ombudsman's records](/blog/prs-ombudsman-landlord-membership-requirements), so it must be active before you begin.

## How Do I Add My Rental Property?

With your landlord account created, you add each property you let. Enter the full address including postcode, and the portal will cross-reference it against the Land Registry and the EPC register to confirm the property exists and pull through basic details automatically.

For each property, confirm:

- The property type (house, flat, HMO, etc.)
- The number of bedrooms
- The current tenancy status and number of occupants

If you have a portfolio, repeat this step for every property. There is no bulk-upload facility at launch, so portfolio landlords should set aside extra time — roughly five to ten minutes per property once your documents are to hand.

## How Do I Enter My Compliance Details?

This is the heart of the registration and the part that catches out landlords who have let their paperwork drift. For each property, you provide the certificate reference numbers and dates for:

- **[Gas safety certificate](/blog/gas-safety-certificate-landlord-guide-2026)** — certificate number, date of issue, and expiry date
- **[Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)](/blog/electrical-safety-certificate-landlord-requirements)** — report reference and outcome (satisfactory or unsatisfactory)
- **[Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)](/blog/epc-rating-requirements-rental-property-2026)** — the Report Reference Number (RRN) from the EPC register and the current rating
- **[Smoke and carbon monoxide alarm compliance](/blog/smoke-carbon-monoxide-alarm-regulations-landlords)** — confirmation that alarms are installed and tested as required
- **[Deposit protection details](/blog/tenant-deposit-protection-rules-landlords)** — the scheme used and the certificate reference

The portal cross-references several of these against national databases automatically, so the details you enter must match the source records exactly. A mistyped EPC reference or an expired gas certificate will flag your registration as incomplete.

<Callout variant="warning">Do not start this step until every certificate is current. If a gas safety certificate or EICR has lapsed, renew it *before* you register — the portal will record the gap, and a lapsed certificate at the point of registration is exactly the kind of evidence a tenant or council can later rely on.</Callout>

## How Do I Review My Registration?

Before submitting, the portal presents a summary of everything you have entered — your details, each property, and all compliance records. Check it carefully. Verify that every certificate number, every date, and every property detail is correct. It is far easier to correct a typo here than to amend a submitted record later.

## How Do I Submit My Registration?

When you are satisfied the summary is accurate, submit your registration. You will receive a confirmation email containing your unique portal registration number. **Save this number and the confirmation email** — you will need the registration number to demonstrate compliance, and you may be asked for it when serving notices or in possession proceedings.

Registration is not a one-off task. When a certificate is renewed, a tenancy changes, or you add a property, you must update the portal. Keeping your records current is itself a legal obligation, not just good practice.

## Who Needs to Register?

Every private landlord who lets residential property in England under an assured tenancy must register. The [government's guidance on renting out a property](https://www.gov.uk/renting-out-a-property) sets out the wider obligations, but for the portal the duty is broad and includes:

- Landlords with a single buy-to-let property
- Portfolio landlords with multiple properties
- Landlords who use a letting agent to manage their properties — the obligation falls on the landlord, not the agent

<Callout variant="warning">Using a managing agent does not exempt you from registration. The legal duty sits with the landlord. Your agent may complete the process on your behalf, but you remain responsible for ensuring it is done and that the information is accurate.</Callout>

## Why the Portal Matters

### For Possession Proceedings

Your ability to [pursue possession through the courts](/blog/how-to-end-tenancy-after-section-21-abolished) depends on your portal registration being up to date. If you are not registered, or your compliance documents have lapsed, the court may refuse to grant a possession order — and a tenant may rely on your non-registration as a defence.

### For Avoiding Penalties

Failing to register, providing false information, or failing to keep records current carries civil penalties of up to £7,000 for a first offence under [Section 21 of the Renters' Rights Act 2025](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/section/21), with higher penalties and potential criminal liability for repeat or serious breaches.

### For Tenant Relations

Tenants can check the portal to verify a landlord's registration and compliance status. A landlord who is fully registered with documents in order signals professionalism; one absent from the portal, or showing lapsed certificates, invites difficult questions.

## How the Portal Interacts with Local Authorities

Local councils have long struggled to identify private landlords — particularly those who operate without letting agents. The portal gives local authorities direct access to a database of every registered landlord and property in their area. In practice this means councils can check compliance proactively rather than waiting for complaints, target enforcement more efficiently, and leave already-compliant landlords largely undisturbed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When exactly must I register on the Property Portal?

All private landlords in England must register by 1 May 2026. The deadline applies whether you have one property or a portfolio, and whether you manage the property yourself or use a letting agent.

### What happens if I don't register by 1 May 2026?

Failure to register can attract civil penalties of up to £7,000 for a first offence, and courts may refuse to grant possession orders while you are unregistered — meaning you cannot regain possession through legal channels until you comply.

### Can my letting agent register on my behalf?

Your agent can complete the process for you, but the legal obligation sits with you as the landlord. The registration must be in your name and the information must be accurate and kept up to date.

### What if I don't have all the compliance documents yet?

Obtain every required document — a valid gas safety certificate, EICR, EPC, smoke and carbon monoxide alarm confirmation, and deposit protection details — *before* registering. Registering with gaps leaves your record incomplete and may expose you to penalties.

### Is there a fee to register on the Property Portal?

Fee details are being confirmed through secondary legislation. Check the [official Property Portal guidance](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/property-portal-landlord-registration-guidance) for the current position before you register.
